Jinja, Uganda

Jinja is located in Uganda, East Africa. It is known to have the source of the world's longest river, the Nile. The city sits on the banks of Africa's largest lake, Lake Victoria. It is an old city built during British colonial rule in the late 19th century. It is known as the adventure capital of East Africa because of the many thrilling activities it offers. These include bungee jumping, white-water rafting, zip-lining, hiking, etc. The city is Uganda's major tourist destination.
